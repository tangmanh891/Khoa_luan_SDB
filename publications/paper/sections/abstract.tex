\begin{abstract}
Shot boundary detection (SBD) is a fundamental preprocessing step for video
indexing, retrieval, summarization, and automated editing. Modern short-video
platforms create a difficult operating regime: clips are brief, edits are
dense, transitions are visually diverse, and boundary frames are sparse. This
paper presents AutoShotV2, a lightweight refinement of AutoShot for
short-video SBD. Rather than re-running neural architecture search or
updating the full backbone, AutoShotV2 freezes the AutoShot feature extractor
and trains a compact classification head on 4864-dimensional cached features.
The resulting logits are then calibrated with temperature scaling, smoothed
with a one-dimensional Gaussian filter, and thresholded under a
validation-selected operating point. With the fixed deployment configuration ($T^{*}=\PaperDeployTemperature$), AutoShotV2 reaches an F1 score of \PaperDeployShotFOne{} on the 200-video SHOT test set, improving over the reproduced AutoShot baseline by \PaperDeployVsAutoShotPP{} percentage points in F1. A supplemental training configuration with per-dataset oracle threshold reaches 0.8607 on SHOT (different checkpoint from the deployed model; reported for potential-analysis only). The same model achieves high performance on BBC documentary footage (\PaperDeployBBCFOne) but remains weaker on open-world ClipShots (\PaperDeployClipFOne). A controlled replication under a frozen
validation-only protocol indicates that the measured gain is driven by
calibrated post-processing rather than by Focal Loss or boundary-aware
many-hot supervision. Paired video bootstrap intervals support positive
deltas on SHOT and ClipShots but not on BBC. Calibration diagnostics further
show that low aggregate frame-level ECE can hide larger positive-class error
under severe boundary sparsity, and a three-seed study observes F1 standard
deviation below 0.5 percentage points on every dataset. We provide
validation-selection manifests, per-video statistics, analysis code, and
checksum-oriented artifact tooling.
\end{abstract}
