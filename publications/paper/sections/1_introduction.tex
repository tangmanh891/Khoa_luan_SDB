\section{Introduction}

Effectively parsing video content is a fundamental requirement for many
downstream applications such as video indexing, content retrieval,
summarization, and automated editing. Within this pipeline, shot boundary
detection (SBD) serves as a critical preliminary step: it partitions a video
into temporally coherent shots by identifying the exact frames where camera
views, scene contents, or editing states transition
\cite{seridi2020survey,kar2024review}. Classical methods based on
hand-crafted cues such as color histograms and motion differences are
efficient but fragile under fast camera motion, flashes, and modern editing
styles \cite{abdulhussain2018review}.

Deep architectures such as TransNet and TransNetV2
\cite{soucek2019transnet,soucek2020transnetv2} learn spatial-temporal
representations directly from frame sequences and have significantly
advanced the state-of-the-art. Concurrently, the rise of short-video
platforms (e.g., TikTok, YouTube Shorts) has introduced a new, challenging
operating regime: brief videos, dense editing, and heavy special effects. To
address it, AutoShot \cite{zhu2023autoshot} leverages neural architecture
search (NAS) to find an optimal 3-D convolutional backbone, demonstrating
strong performance on the newly introduced SHOT dataset. Further improving
these models, however, encounters a severe bottleneck: the NAS pipeline
requires immense computational overhead for supernet training and
architecture evaluation. Moreover, short-video SBD suffers from extreme
class imbalance --- boundary frames are exceptionally rare --- and complex
visual effects that act as hard negatives.

Following the strategy of separating representation learning from
task-specific adaptation, we study a more efficient refinement path for
short-video SBD. Instead of re-running the expensive NAS process, we freeze
the searched AutoShot backbone and improve only the decision stage, a method
we term AutoShotV2. Its core component is a dual-branch classification head
applied over the frozen 4864-dimensional cached features. To handle the
class imbalance, we explore Focal Loss \cite{lin2017focal} on the primary
branch and a boundary-aware many-hot supervision strategy on the auxiliary
branch, providing denser temporal gradients without compromising the precise
one-hot localization of the primary branch. Finally, because SBD decisions
rely directly on thresholding model scores, we introduce a calibrated
post-processing stage: temperature scaling \cite{guo2017calibration}
adjusts the numerical scale of the predictions without altering their
ranking, and a one-dimensional Gaussian filter smooths out isolated
false-positive peaks caused by sudden flashes or camera shakes.

With the fixed deployment configuration, AutoShotV2 achieves an F1-score of
\PaperDeployShotFOne{} on the SHOT test set, improving over the reproduced
AutoShot baseline and the reported TransNetV2 baseline by
\PaperDeployVsAutoShotPP{} and \PaperDeployVsTransNetPP{} F1 points,
respectively. A controlled replication under a frozen, validation-only
five-fold protocol disentangles the sources of this gain: the training-loss
alternatives change F1 only marginally, whereas calibrated post-processing
adds \PaperBFourVsAOneShotPP{} F1 points on SHOT and
\PaperBFourVsAOneClipPP{} points on ClipShots over the BCE control, with
paired bootstrap intervals confirming the deltas on SHOT and ClipShots but
not BBC.

In summary, our main contributions are:
\begin{itemize}
\item We propose AutoShotV2, an efficient SBD pipeline that improves
short-video boundary detection without the computational burden of
retraining the NAS-searched backbone, reaching \PaperDeployShotFOne{} F1 on
SHOT with a fixed deployment configuration.
\item We design a two-branch classification head and systematically explore
Focal Loss and boundary-aware many-hot supervision as a training design
space, showing through a controlled ablation that calibrated post-processing
--- not the more complex losses --- drives the measured gain.
\item We define a validation-only, source-stratified selection protocol that
freezes temperature, smoothing, and threshold before any test evaluation,
and quantify uncertainty with paired video bootstrap intervals and
calibration diagnostics including adaptive and class-balanced ECE.
\item We provide extensive experiments on SHOT, BBC, and ClipShots with
strict cache identities, exact split/run manifests, a three-seed robustness
study, and checksum-based artifact packaging.
\end{itemize}

The paper reports results on two tracks by design: the deployed checkpoint
supplies the headline numbers and is preserved as tracked, checksummed
result files, while a controlled replication with full logit caches anchors
every confidence interval, calibration diagnostic, and ablation
(Section~\ref{sec:tracks}).
